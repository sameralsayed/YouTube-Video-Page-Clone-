Does the universe have a beginning? Does it have an end? Philosophers, religious figures, alchemists, and scientists have grappled with these questions. For over two thousand years, Aristotle's ideas about the "eternity of the world" dominated. Then, in the early 20th century, we accidentally discovered that our galaxy isn't the only one, and all galaxies are moving away from us at speeds depending on their distance. This led to the idea of the "expanding universe," which might answer whether the universe has a beginning and an end.

In today's episode, we discover that the universe, like us, has a birth, adolescence, youth, and eventually death and decay; stars, planets, galaxies, even protons, cosmic gases, and black holes die in it, and in the end, no matter remains after its energy is exhausted and dissipates completely.
